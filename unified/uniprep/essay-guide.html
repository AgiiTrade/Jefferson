📝 Common App Essay Prompts (2025-2026)

1. Some students have a background, identity, interest, or talent that is so meaningful they believe their application would be incomplete without it.

If this sounds like you, then please share your story.

650 words max

2. The lessons we take from obstacles we encounter can be fundamental to later success.

Recount a time when you faced a challenge, setback, or failure. How did it affect you, and what did you learn from the experience?

650 words max

3. Reflect on a time when you questioned or challenged a belief or idea.

What prompted your thinking? What was the outcome?

650 words max

4. Reflect on something that someone has done for you that has made you happy or thankful in a surprising way.

How has that gratitude affected or motivated you?

650 words max

5. Discuss an accomplishment, event, or realization that sparked a period of personal growth and a new understanding of yourself or others.

650 words max

6. Describe a topic, idea, or concept you find so engaging that it makes you lose all track of time.

Why does it captivate you? What or who do you turn to when you want to learn more?

650 words max

7. Share an essay on any topic of your choice.

It can be one you've already written, one that responds to a different prompt, or one of your own design.

650 words max

💡 Essay Writing Tips

  • Show, don't tell: Use specific anecdotes and details instead of generic statements
  • Be authentic: Write in your own voice, don't try to sound impressive
  • Start strong: Your opening paragraph should hook the reader immediately
  • Focus on growth: Admissions officers want to see how you've changed or learned
  • Avoid clichés: Sports injury, mission trip, immigration story — unless told exceptionally well
  • Be specific: Name the school, professor, program when writing "Why Us?" essays
  • Proofread: Have at least 2-3 people review your essays before submitting
